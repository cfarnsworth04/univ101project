UNIV 101 & the Aims of BYU

How a first-year experience course supports a BYU education.

Organized around the four Aims of a BYU Education.

“Education is the power to think clearly, the power to act well in the world’s work, and the power to appreciate life.”

— B.H. Roberts

UNIV 101 is designed to help new students transition into BYU life. It strengthens spiritual, intellectual, and personal foundations while inviting students into a life of discipleship, learning, and service.

Click each aim to expand and see how UNIV 101 supports it.

  • Helps students understand and value the spiritual mission of BYU.
  • Introduces gospel-centered learning and the disciple-scholar model.
  • Encourages reflection on how faith integrates with education.

“Seek learning, even by study and also by faith.”

— Doctrine & Covenants 88:118

  • Helps students understand the purpose of general education and broad learning.
  • Teaches strategies for academic success and effective use of resources.
  • Encourages intellectual humility and curiosity.

“The university is not only a place for the transmission of knowledge but the creation of knowledge.”

— President Spencer W. Kimball, “Education for Eternity”

  • Emphasizes integrity, responsibility, and respectful dialogue.
  • Builds habits of self-management, resilience, and personal growth.
  • Connects students with BYU’s expectations for conduct and belonging.

“Character is the highest aim of education.”

— President David O. McKay

  • Helps students set long-term goals and see education as ongoing growth.
  • Encourages service-oriented mindsets and contribution to community.
  • Shows how the BYU experience prepares students for lifelong discipleship.

“True education does not end with commencement. It is a lifelong pursuit of truth and service.”

— President Gordon B. Hinckley (paraphrased)
